Emotional Intelligence

Mindfulness is something I've been pretty interested in and actively working on for the past two years. What I hadn't really come across before was the analytical reasoning behind it displayed in Search Inside Yourself. I think its interesting that the author chose to examine the neurological causes and changes behind it. Probably this is the best way to get software engineers who are used to thinking very rationally to understand mindfulness in an objective field.


The two minutes of mindfulness was a good jump back into mindfulness meditation which is something I always want to do but keep earmarking for the next day, or the day after that. I should start trying to form two minutes of mindfulness as a habit, probably in terms of forced breaks at EDA as I think I haven't been taking enough breaks at the moment.


My general thoughts on mindfulness is that it needs to be taught on a much wider scale, ideally to kids starting school. I think this is being tested in some schools in isolation and showing great results. It's importance is so understated though. I think so many detrimental behaviours in the world from both individuals and organizations would cease immediately through mindfulness, and not even mastery of mindfulness, just a simple base level. But instead you have individuals who aren't necessarily bad people on an objective level but have a low level of awareness and mindfulness carrying out harmful ego-driven behaviour while lacking the emotional intelligence to even consider the effects of their behaviour on others. /rant
